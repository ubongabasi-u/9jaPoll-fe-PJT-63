    Chris Okotie

    Babatunde sanwo Olu

    Babajide Olusola Sanwo-Olu (born June 25, 1965) is a Nigerian politician and the current Governor of Lagos State. He became governor under the platform of the All Progressives Congress after contesting and unexpectedly winning the gubernatorial primaries under the All Progressives Congress against incumbent Governor Akinwunmi Ambode of Lagos State in October 2019. He is a graduate of the University of Lagos, as well as the London Business School, Lagos Business School and the John F. Kennedy School of Government.

    Babajide Olusola Sanwo-Olu was the treasurer at the former Lead Merchant Bank from 1994 to 1997 after which he moved to the United Bank for Africa as the head of foreign money market. He then proceeded to First Inland Bank, Plc (now First City Monument Bank) as a deputy general manager and divisional head. He was the chairman of Baywatch Group Limited and First Class Group Limited.

    Before his gubernatorial ambition, he was the managing director and CEO of Lagos State Property Development Corporation (LSPDC).

    willie obiano

    Obiano was born on the 8th of August, 1955 to the family of Phillip Obiano and Christiana Obiano in Anambra State, Nigeria. He originally hails from Aguleri but was raised in Onitsha, where he also had his primary and secondary education.

    For his primary education, he attended Holy Trinity School, after which he enrolled at the Christ the King College (CKC). Obiano holds a degree in Accounting and an MBA in marketing, both from the prestigious University of Lagos (UniLag).

    In 1981, Willie Obiano began his professional career at the First Bank of Nigeria. He left FirstBank to join Chevron Oil Nigeria Plc as an accountant, rising to the position of Chief Internal Auditor.

    In 1991, he joined the Fidelity Bank as Deputy Manager and head of Audit unit and rose to the position of Executive Director in October 2003.

    In 2013, Obiano left the banking system for politics.He contested the Anambra gubernatorial elections under the platform of the All Progressives Grand Alliance (APGA) and won, succeeding Peter Obi. He was re-elected to the seat on November 18, 2017.

    jimi agbaje

    Olujimi Kolawole Agbaje (popularly known as Jimi Agbaje or JK; born 2 March 1957), is a Nigerian Pharmacist, and Politician. He was the 2015 PDP's Lagos State Governorship candidate, but he lost out to the eventual winner Akinwunmi Ambode. He was the 2019 Lagos State gubernatorial election candidate for PDP in the 2019 general elections.

    A consummate professional, Jimi Agbaje founded his own company, JAYKAY Pharmaceutical and Chemical Company Limited and also sits on the Board of other organizations including Oakwood Park Ltd, Atlantic Hall Secondary School Epe, Lagos, Jimi Agbaje Outreach (a project dedicated to helping the less fortunate) and has served as business mentor at Fate Foundation.

    Jimi Agbaje secured the PDP's ticket to run for the office of the governor of Lagos State by defeating Adedeji Doherty. However, he lost to Babajide Sanwo.
